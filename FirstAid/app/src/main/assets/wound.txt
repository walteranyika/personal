<h2><font color="#4CAF50">First Aid for Wounds</font></h2>
<font color="#F44336">Minor wounds </font><font color="#4CAF50">include abrasions, lacerations, punctures and incisions. 
The most significant issues to consider with any open wound are control of bleeding and infection.</font>

<h4><font color="#4CAF50">First Aid:</font></h4>
<p><font color="#3F51B5">If bleeding, apply direct pressure with a clean cloth or absorbent pad.</font><p>
<p><font color="#3F51B5">Wash area with antibacterial soap and clean until there appears to be no foreign matter in the wound.</font><p>
<p><font color="#3F51B5">Cover area with an adhesive bandage or gauze wrap.</font><p>


<p><font color="#F44336">Crush Injury:</font> Occurs when a body part is subjected to a high degree of force or pressure . 
Example: smashed fingers in door.<p>

<h4><font color="#4CAF50">First Aid:</font></h4>
<p><font color="#3F51B5">Apply ice just as you would with a bruising injury.
If pain is severe and does not lessen with ice or there is decreased sensation, weakness, or paleness of the skin in the affected area, seek emergency care.</font><p>

<p><font color="#F44336">Bruising: </font><font color="#3F51B5">Caused by broken blood vessels leaking blood under the skin. 
Bruising can be minimal or large and severe.</font><p>

<h4><font color="#4CAF50">First Aid</font></h4>
<p><font color="#3F51B5">Apply ice to injury to reduce pain, bleeding and swelling.
To prevent frost bite to the injured area, place a thin towel or cloth between the skin and ice. 
Limit ice application to 20 minutes on, 20 off.</font><p>
