<h2><font color="#4CAF50">First aid for diabetes</font></h2>
<h4><font color="#F44336">Key skill: Give them something sweet to drink or eat</font></h4>
<p>1. Give them something sweet to eat or a non-diet drink.<p>
<p>2. Reassure the person.If there is no improvement,or get someone else to do it.<p>


<h4><font color="#F44336">Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)</font></h4>

<h4><font color="#4CAF50">Signs and Symptoms:</font></h4>
<p>1. Shakiness<p>
<p>2. Dizziness<p>
<p>3. Sweating<p>
<p>4. Hunger<p>
<p>5. Irritability or moodiness<p>
<p>6. Anxiety or nervousness<p>
<p>7. Headache, vision problems<p>
<p>8. Drowsiness<p>
<p>9. Seizures<p>

<h4><font color="#F44336">First Aid Interventions:</font></h4>
<p>1. Five to six pieces of hard candy<p>
<p>2. Four ounces of fruit juice or regular,not diet, soda<p>
<p>3. One tablespoon of sugar, jelly or honey<p>
<p>4. Three glucose tablets (available without a prescription at most pharmacies)<p>
